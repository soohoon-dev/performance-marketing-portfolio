## Featured Projects

### 1. Community Needs Assessment (2019)

Conducted comprehensive health survey to inform program priorities and identify barriers to preventive care in the Korean-American community.

**Project Scope:**

- Designed bilingual survey instrument (English/Korean)
- Surveyed 34 respondents (ages 20-80+)
- Analyzed data and created 17-page report with visualizations
- Presented findings and recommendations to program team

**Key Findings:**

- **Healthcare access gaps:** 44% had not received comprehensive checkup in 2+ years; 35% had not received blood testing in 2+ years
- **Top health concerns:** Chronic fatigue (12 mentions), high cholesterol (8), joint issues (9)
- **Cancer screening interests:** Cervical (10), breast (9), colorectal (9), stomach (9), lung (9)
- **Mental health priorities:** Stress management (13 mentions), depression (8), anger management (6)

**Impact:**
Survey insights directly informed 2019-2020 program development, including colorectal cancer screening initiatives, mental health seminar series, and partnership strategy with Cedars-Sinai for cancer screening resources.

### 2. Blood Testing & Flu Shot Clinic (November 2021)

Coordinated large-scale dual-service health event with three medical partners, serving 171 total services to underserved community members.

**Event Scope:**

- **Participants:** 87 blood tests, 84 flu shots (42 received both services)
- **Partners:** LA County Public Health (6 staff), 3C Clinic (5 staff), Cedars-Sinai (1 coordinator)
- **Volunteers:** 12 community volunteers + medical students
- **Budget:** $6,295

**Key Deliverables:**

- 17-page operational plan with venue layouts, participant flow diagrams, volunteer assignments, and logistics checklists
- Multi-station layout design with COVID-19 safety protocols
- Bilingual registration system (English/Korean)
- Volunteer recruitment, training, and role assignments
- Budget management across testing services ($5,510), meals ($535), supplies ($250)

**Logistics Managed:**

- Parking coordination for 11 partner vehicles
- Power supply setup for medical equipment (vaccine refrigeration, computers)
- Venue setup: 7 tables, shade structures, socially-distanced waiting areas
- Meal service for 25 staff/volunteers
- Participant materials distribution

**Outcomes:**
171 total services delivered on schedule with zero wait time issues; expanded outreach to neighboring Latino community through bilingual promotion; positive feedback from all stakeholders.

### 3. Cancer Screening & Health Passport (2022)

Designed and produced 44-page bilingual health education booklet to improve cancer screening awareness and personal health tracking.

**Project Overview:**

- **Format:** 5.5" x 8.5" saddle-stitch booklet, 44 pages
- **Distribution:** 500+ copies to Korean-American households
- **Funding:** Cedars-Sinai Cancer Center grant ($3,000)
- **Timeline:** 3 months (research, design, stakeholder review, production)

**Content Developed:**

- Cancer screening guidelines for 5 cancer types (breast, colorectal, cervical, prostate, lung) based on American Cancer Society and USPSTF recommendations
- Personal health tracking templates (screening records, blood pressure logs, vaccination schedules)
- Free screening program information and eligibility criteria
- Community clinic contact directory
- 7 evidence-based cancer prevention strategies

**Design & Production:**

- Information architecture and bilingual layout (English/Korean)
- Icon and graphic development for screening timelines
- Multiple stakeholder review cycles with Cedars-Sinai medical staff
- Print vendor management and quality control
- Distribution coordination

**Tools Used:** Adobe InDesign (layout), Illustrator (graphics), plain language health communication principles

**Impact:** 500+ booklets distributed; ongoing use as educational resource at community events; positive partner feedback; replicable model for future materials.
